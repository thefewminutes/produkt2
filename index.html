Witness to the Evolution of the Internet

I've seen a lot of changes in the industry during the 11 years of my career as a Web Developer.

While working at a Development firm called IMG2 in 2000 a team of Macromedia Software Engineers came to visit and question us about using their Dreamweaver product. Macromedia had recently acquired Elemental Software, creators of the forward thinking Web Authoring Application Drumbeat 2000. Drumbeat 2000 used an innovative development paradigm that separated structure from presentation and scripting using early CSS, Javascript, database connectivity and html to create dynamic websites. Macromedia in their wisdom had decided to kill Drumbeat 2000 and promote Dreamweaver - their previously static html-only Authoring Application - as their product for developing dynamic, database-driven websites. Dreamweaver would now be offered in 2 flavors: the static html version and the extended version called Dreamweaver Ultradev; an ironic hat-tip to Microsoft's Visual Interdev. In spite of Drumbeat 2000's brilliant development paradigm Macromedia's Engineers were championing html tables as the presentational layout standard and workaround for browser compatibility issues even though it made no sense (a layout is not tabular data). Macromedia foresaw an Internet full of table layouts and font tags, and their vision prevailed for a few years until the w3c came to the rescue.
